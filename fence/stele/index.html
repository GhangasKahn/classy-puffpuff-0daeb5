01 — The creed

Three empires, one lesson: mass, water, geometry

Old-world structures survive because they obey physics, not fashion. Each civilization contributes one load-bearing idea to this fence.

𓉴

Egypt

The batter · talus

Every pier face leans inward at 1:26.7 — the pylon taper. The resultant of wind plus self-weight stays inside the middle third of every bed joint, so no joint ever sees tension. Tension is what frost and time exploit; the batter removes it.

⌂

Rome

The foundation · the arch

Footings bear 48″ down on compacted stone — below Buffalo frost, drained so the base never sits wet. The gate spans as a pure-compression semicircular arch: eleven voussoirs and a keystone that needs no steel to stand.

▲

Maya

The platform · the corbel

Everything precious rides on a raised plinth, clear of splash and snowpack. Pier tops stack in corbelled tiers with drip kerfs — a stepped pyramid in miniature that sheds ice instead of holding it.

02 — Buffalo criteria

Designed against Erie County weather

Numbers the geometry answers to. Verify the height limits of your Buffalo Green Code district before digging.

Frost
Footing base 1220 mm (48″) below grade, on 150 mm compacted #57 stone — below Erie County design frost depth.
Snow
40 psf ground snow + lake-effect drift. Timber held 250 mm above grade on a masonry curb; caps shed on 1:12 washes.
Wind
115 mph ultimate (ASCE 7). Pier self-weight ≥ 3× panel wind reaction; battered mass resists overturning without straps.
Freeze-thaw
Air-entrained 4000 psi concrete, drip kerfs on cap tiers, capillary break between stem and plinth, no water-trapping horizontals.
Heave isolation
Curb wall floats independent of pier footings — if the shallow curb moves, the piers don't care.
Code
Panel top 1825 mm ≤ 6′-0″ residential limit. Pier caps 1965 mm; arch crown 2640 mm — confirm gate/arbor allowance for your district.

06 — Build sequence

Foundations first, arch last

Phase 01

Layout & excavation

String the run, stake 6 pier centers (8′-0″ typical, 1700 mm at the gate). Dig to 54″: 48″ frost + 6″ gravel. Call 811 before any digging.

Phase 02

Foundations

Compact gravel pads, pour footings and stems monolithic where possible. Cure 7 days. Damp-proof stem tops and set the capillary-break flashing.

  • Air-entrained mix, 5–7% — non-negotiable in freeze-thaw country
  • Slope all surrounding grade away at 5%
Phase 03

Piers & curb

Lay battered drums or CMU-plus-veneer, checking taper with a story-pole template. Set cap tiers in Type S with kerfs down. Pour curbs floating between piers.

Phase 04

Timber bays

Slide rails into pockets, wedge-lock; hang boards with gaps; fit sloped caps. All fastening is rail-to-board — masonry stays unpierced above the flashing line.

Phase 05

The arch

Erect timber centering, lay 11 voussoirs to the keystone, cure 7 days, strike centering, hang the leaf. The moment the keystone seats, the gate becomes the strongest bay on the line.

# full pipeline: model + renders + plan sheets
./scripts/build_stele.sh

# plans only
python3 scripts/gen_stele_plans.py
