02 — Three ideas, one fence

Prairie face. Temple joints. Removable feet.

Prairie · Darwin Martin

Horizontal bands

Three continuous 2×8 rails on edge read as Prairie belt courses. Cap rail unbroken across the privacy run. Vertical 1×6 boards recess between bands — privacy without visual noise.

Japan · hand joinery

Nuki + kusabi

Rails pass through the posts and lock with hardwood wedges. Gate uses drawbored mortise & tenon. Cap scarfs with kama-tsugi over the mid post. No nails, no screws, no plates in the timber.

Buffalo · winter logic

Lift and store

Posts drop into sleeved socket piers on a leveling pad that kills the driveway drop-off. Knock wedges, withdraw rails, lift posts, tip piers. Store dry until spring.

06 — Build sequence

Cut dry. Fit. Paint. Set. Drop in.

Phase 1

Site & pad

Measure the 143″ opening and the drop-off. Compact #57 gravel. Form and pour the leveling pad (top dead level) with four pier pockets / set precast socket piers. Sleeve drain holes at the bottom. Cure before loading.

Phase 2

Posts & mortises

Cut four 4×6 blanks to 77″ (65″ above + 12″ tenon). Shoulder the foot tenons. Cut through-mortises for R1–R3. Cut wedge slots. Dry-fit tenons into sleeves.

Phase 3

Rails & boards

Mill 2×8 nuki rails; plow ⅜″ board grooves. Cut 1×6 boards to course heights. Assemble privacy run on sawhorses — rails through posts, boards dropped in, wedges tapped. Cap with kama-tsugi over P2, drawbored.

Phase 4

Gate & latch

Build leaf with drawbored hozo joints and half-lap brace. Fit wooden pintles on P1. Install sliding oak latch — into house concrete receiver (Latch A) or P0 mortise (Latch B). Confirm swing arc.

Phase 5

Paint & set

Disassemble for paint or paint in place with wedges pulled. Primer + two coats owner gray. Mask all locking faces. Drop posts into sleeves, re-insert rails and wedges, hang gate, lock.

  • Winter reverse: wedges → rails → boards → gate → posts → tip piers → store dry
  • Spring: reverse again; re-seat wedges snug, not brutal
