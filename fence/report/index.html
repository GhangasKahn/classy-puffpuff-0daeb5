1Executive summary

This report delivers a buildable, winter-removable fence for a 143″ × 65″ site opening: FreeCAD model, dimensioned blueprints, cut list with board feet, joinery vocabulary, leveling pad for the driveway drop-off, and a locking privacy gate.

The design problem. The existing fence is 143″ long, 65″ high, and ¾″ thick. It must become freestanding and solid in Buffalo wind, frost, and wet–dry cycling; include a locking privacy gate (hooking into house concrete or self-latching); sit level across a driveway→garden drop; and come apart so the timber (and tip-out piers) can be stored for winter. Aesthetic: Prairie School / Darwin Martin horizontals, built entirely with Japanese hand joinery from hardware-store dimensional lumber, painted owner gray — prefer 2×6 / 2×8 over 2×4, no nails or screws in the timber.

The answer — MARTIN (primary for this site):

  • Four 4×6 posts on tip-out socket piers set in a leveled concrete pad that absorbs the grade drop.
  • Three continuous 2×8 Prairie nuki bands + 2×8 cap; 1×6 (¾″) vertical privacy boards floating in grooves.
  • 36″ gate with wooden pintles and sliding oak latch into a house-concrete receiver (or latch post).
  • Knock-down winter sequence: wedges → rails → boards → gate → posts → tip piers.

Related systems archived in this suite: STELE (permanent masonry-pier fence for longer runs) and HASHIRA (standalone joinery reinforcement method). Use them if you later extend beyond 143″ or reinforce another fence.

Status. Geometry is fully modeled. Upload site photos (drop-off height, house latch wall, gray swatch) to lock makeup thickness and receiver detail. Not a stamped PE document — confirm Green Code limits and engineer review if the city requires it.

5System C — HASHIRA (timber joinery vocabulary)

The joinery vocabulary inside STELE's bays — and a standalone method for reinforcing an existing wood fence without tearing it down.

6Engineering rationale

Why MARTIN stands in wind without nails

Wind on a 65″ × 143″ face delivers shear and overturning into the posts. Through-rails (nuki) turn that shear into wood-on-wood compression inside the posts; wedges keep the rails from walking. Overturning is resisted by the mass of each socket pier plus the 12″ tenon couple. There is no nail withdrawal path to fatigue in freeze-thaw.

Why the pad matters

A fence that is not level racks its joints on day one. The leveling pad creates a single plane across the driveway→garden drop so every post bears evenly. Makeup thickness is a field dimension — default 5″ until site photos confirm.

Why winter removal is structural, not a compromise

Buffalo snowplows and ice berms destroy fences that stay at the curb. Designing the piers as tip-out modules and the timber as wedge-locked knock-down means the fence survives by leaving. The pad can stay as a low curb; everything precious goes indoors.

STELE archive notes (Egypt / Rome / Maya)

For permanent longer runs, STELE's battered piers keep bed joints in compression, 48″ drained footings beat frost, and raised curbs keep wood out of snowpack — see the STELE design page.

Failure-mode audit (MARTIN)

Wind racking → nuki compression + pier mass. Grade drop → leveling pad. Rot → elevation + paint + drained sleeves. Fastener corrosion → no fasteners in timber. Plow impact → pad sacrificial, timber stored. Theft / privacy → locking latch into house concrete or P0.

10Permits & code path — Buffalo

  • Confirm parcel zoning in the Buffalo Green Code — residential fences commonly limited to 6′ side/rear; 65″ panel height is designed to comply. Front-yard limits can be lower — verify before building.
  • Removable freestanding fences are often treated more lightly than permanent masonry, but ask Permit & Inspection Services if a permit is required for the pad.
  • Call 811 (Dig Safely New York) before any excavation for the pad/piers.
  • If latching into house concrete, confirm you are drilling into foundation/wall you own; use epoxy sleeve carefully around waterproofing.
  • Historic district rules if applicable (Darwin Martin neighborhood context — check).
  • This package is not a stamped engineering document. Hire a NY PE if the city requests one.
